description Description

MAGset is a tool designed to analyze metagenome-assembled genomes (MAGs) of known species that have published reference genomes. Its primary goal is to identify the differences between a specific MAG (classified at the species level) and one or more reference genomes by determining the genomic regions of interest (GRIs).

indeterminate_question_box What is a Genomic Region of Interest (GRI)?

Genomic Region of Interest (GRI) is a concept created in this project to define sequence regions of at least 5000 bases (default value) that exist in one genome but do not exist in another. GRIs are divided in two types:

  • Positive GRIs: Region found in MAG genome, but not found in any reference genome;
  • Negative GRIs: Region not found in MAG genome, but found in one or more reference genomes;

You can use the module MAGcheck to search for negative GRIs in your raw data (the data used to assembly the MAG). If any negative GRIs were found, it may suggest that your assembly process lost parts of the genome, and if you want a more complete MAG, it may be a good idea reassemble it.
